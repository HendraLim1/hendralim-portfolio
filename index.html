About

The path less conventional.

I didn't take the straight path into tech — and that's exactly what makes me effective.

I moved from Indonesia to Sydney to study civil engineering at UTS, where I earned a Dean's Merit Award and was invited to their Leadership Program. When ChatGPT launched and AI began reshaping every industry, I saw the future clearly: technology isn't just a career — it's the foundation everything else is being built on.

So I pivoted. I completed a Bachelor of IT in Cyber Security at Kent Institute, graduating on the Dean's List. While studying, I led teams as Shift Manager at McDonald's and Shift Supervisor at Starbucks — learning to manage people under pressure, solve problems in real time, and deliver results when it matters.

Today, I'm embedded in a full-scale enterprise IT transformation at Australian Plastic Profiles, now part of Legrand. My title says IT Assistant, but the work spans infrastructure, cloud migration, networking, Active Directory, and compliance audit preparation.
