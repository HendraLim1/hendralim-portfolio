My Story

The path less conventional

I didn't take the straight path into tech — and that's exactly what makes me effective.

I moved from Indonesia to Sydney to study civil engineering at UTS, where I earned a Dean's Merit Award and was invited to their Leadership Program. But when ChatGPT launched and AI began reshaping every industry, I saw the future clearly: technology isn't just a career — it's the foundation everything else is being built on.

So I pivoted. I completed a Bachelor of IT in Cyber Security at Kent Institute, graduating on the Dean's List. While studying, I worked as a Shift Manager at McDonald's and a Shift Supervisor at Starbucks — not just to pay the bills, but learning to lead teams under pressure, solve problems in real-time, and deliver results when it matters.

Today, I'm the person who turns complexity into clarity. At Australian Plastic Profiles (now part of Legrand), I'm not just an IT Assistant — I'm hands-on in a full enterprise transformation: migrating on-prem servers to Azure, deploying network infrastructure, transitioning SAP B1 to the cloud, and preparing for internal audits. I thrive when things are messy and need to be made right.

Outside of work, I serve as Vice Chair of the ACS Emerging Professional Committee, where I plan events, lead meetings, and MC conferences — because I believe in giving back to the community that welcomed me.
